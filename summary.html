In Civil Appeal No. 340 of 20 02.03.2015, the Supreme Court of Pakistan addressed the case of Muhammad Anwar, who sought leave to appeal against the Lahore High Court's order dated 08.12.1999, which convicted him of qatl-i-amd (murder) and sentenced him to death under Section 302(b) of the Pakistan Penal Code (PPC). The appeal was allowed, removing the impugned judgment of the Lahore High Court regarding the Qatl-i-amd (murder) case.The Supreme Court found that the prosecution failed to prove the charges beyond reasonable doubt, as they were based on unsubstantiated allegations of qatl-i-amd (murder) against his wife, Mst. Azhra Parveen, and their minor son Waqar. The Court emphasized that the Qatl-i-amd (murder) charge did not apply to the Qatl-i-amd case, as the primary accusation was that of qatl-i-amd (murder) against his wife.The Court found that the prosecution failed to prove the charges beyond reasonable doubt, leading to the conclusion that Anwar was entitled to the benefit of Section 302(b) and the death of the children. Consequently, the Supreme Court dismissed the appeal, affirming the Lahore High Court's decision to detain Anwar under Section 308 PPC for further proceedings..
